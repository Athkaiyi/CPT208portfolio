SyncValley A Gamified Platform for Engaging Group Dynamics

SyncValley transforms student group work into a cozy pixel-art RPG experience. It is designed to reduce the free-rider problem by combining AI-assisted task breakdown, quest-based collaboration, visible contribution tracking, and playful rewards that make teamwork fairer, clearer, and more motivating.

Problem Unequal participation in student group projects
Approach Gameful cooperation with AI-supported task guidance
Goal Fairer, more engaging, and less stressful teamwork

02 · MOTIVATION

Why We Chose the Social Track

We chose the social track because unfair participation in student teamwork is a common and emotionally stressful issue in university life. In many group projects, some students contribute actively while others remain passive, delay communication, or only participate near the deadline. This “free-rider” problem creates resentment among committed members, weakens team trust, and often makes collaboration feel more like conflict management than shared learning.

The problem is also complex because passive participation does not always come from laziness. Some students may feel overwhelmed, uncertain about what to do, anxious about communicating, or disconnected from the project. Therefore, simply exposing low contribution is not enough. A meaningful design response should help students understand their tasks, feel encouraged to participate, and recognise progress before the project reaches a crisis point.

SyncValley responds to this challenge by combining practical project coordination with playful motivation. Through a pixel-art RPG metaphor, the platform makes collaboration feel less intimidating. Through AI task breakdown, it turns vague project goals into manageable quests. Through contribution visibility and progression rewards, it supports fairer participation while keeping the experience supportive rather than punitive.

03 · RESEARCH

Understanding the Collaboration Problem

Our research combined survey-based insights, interviews, user observation, and literature review. The goal was to understand not only what happens in unfair group work, but also why students disengage and what forms of support could make group collaboration more balanced.

📋

Questionnaire

We collected student perspectives on workload fairness, contribution visibility, task clarity, and emotional stress during collaborative coursework.

🎙️

Interviews

We explored personal stories about free-riding, conflict avoidance, delayed responses, and the difficulty of asking teammates to contribute more.

👀

Observation

We observed how students assign tasks, track progress, negotiate responsibilities, and react when group members become inactive.

📚

Literature Review

We reviewed work on collaborative learning, gamification, peer evaluation, motivation, and AI-supported educational systems.

04 · REVIEW

The Gap in Existing Solutions

Existing productivity tools such as Notion, Trello, Asana, Discord, and Microsoft Teams support communication and task organisation, but they do not directly address the emotional and motivational dimensions of unequal student collaboration. They can show what tasks exist, but they rarely help disengaged students understand where to start, or help active students feel that contribution is being recognised fairly.

Academic research on collaborative learning, peer assessment, gamification, and AI-supported education provides useful principles, but many research systems remain conceptual, difficult to adopt, or insufficiently playful for everyday student teamwork. This gap motivated SyncValley: a tool that combines the structure of a task management system with the emotional engagement of a cooperative RPG experience.

Academic Papers

  • Did well: explain peer collaboration and motivation mechanisms.
  • Did well: provide evidence for structured feedback and assessment.
  • Missed: playful everyday interaction for student teams.
  • Missed: low-anxiety guidance for disengaged participants.

Task Management Tools

  • Did well: organise tasks, deadlines, and project boards.
  • Did well: improve visibility of what needs to be completed.
  • Missed: intrinsic motivation and emotional engagement.
  • Missed: intervention before free-riding becomes serious.

Communication Platforms

  • Did well: support quick team discussion and file sharing.
  • Did well: create convenient channels for updates.
  • Missed: task accountability and contribution transparency.
  • Missed: structured progression through project milestones.

SyncValley Opportunity

  • Make collaboration feel like a shared adventure.
  • Turn vague work into manageable AI-generated quests.
  • Use progress and rewards to encourage continued engagement.
  • Support fairer contribution visibility without harsh punishment.

07 · REQUIREMENTS

Playful Design Requirements

From research and journey mapping, we defined three core design requirements. These requirements helped us evaluate whether the platform was not only functional, but meaningfully playful and socially supportive.

R1

Low-Anxiety Interaction

The interface should reduce pressure and make group work feel approachable through cozy visuals, supportive language, and clear onboarding.

R2

Meaningful Progression

Students should feel visible progress through quests, milestones, rewards, and completion states linked to real project work.

R3

Fair Social Engagement

The system should encourage balanced participation by making contribution visible, supporting early intervention, and avoiding harsh public shaming.

09 · IDEATION

From Alternatives to Final Direction

During ideation, we explored multiple design alternatives. A conventional productivity dashboard offered clarity but lacked emotional engagement. An AI chatbot assistant provided guidance but did not create enough shared team visibility. The final RPG-inspired concept was selected because it combined practical coordination with motivation, shared progress, and a stronger playful identity.

Alternative 1 — Productivity Dashboard

Strong for organising deadlines and task lists, but too similar to existing tools and not playful enough to address motivation.

Alternative 2 — AI Chatbot Assistant

Useful for answering questions and generating suggestions, but weaker in shared visibility and team-wide progression.

Alternative 3 — Pixel RPG Collaboration

Selected direction. It connects AI guidance, quests, contribution tracking, rewards, and a memorable pixel-art experience.

11 · TECHNICAL IMPLEMENTATION

System Architecture

The technical concept connects user input, project data, AI-supported task generation, contribution tracking, and front-end feedback. A user enters or updates project information; the system stores task and progress data; AI support helps convert large goals into smaller quests; and the interface visualises team progress, rewards, and contribution status.

12 · EVALUATION & REFLECTION

Testing, Iteration, and Ethical Reflection

We tested the alpha prototype with student participants using scenario-based tasks. Participants were asked to understand the project space, interpret the quest system, identify contribution feedback, and explain how the platform might affect their motivation in real group work.

Feedback showed that the RPG metaphor was memorable and made collaboration feel less intimidating. Users especially liked the idea of turning large assignments into smaller quests. However, some participants were initially unsure how rewards were calculated and how contribution indicators should be interpreted. In response, we simplified labels, improved onboarding explanations, and made progress states more visible.

The project also raised important ethical considerations. Contribution tracking must be designed carefully to avoid over-surveillance, public shaming, or unfair judgement. AI should support task clarity and reflection, but it should not replace human communication or become the only authority on whether someone has contributed fairly. Therefore, SyncValley frames AI as a supportive guide rather than a final evaluator.

What Worked

The playful RPG theme made the concept memorable and helped users feel that teamwork could be more engaging.

What Needed Improvement

Users needed clearer explanations of reward rules, contribution status, and AI-generated task suggestions.

Iteration Focus

We improved onboarding, refined progress labels, and made contribution feedback more transparent and less punitive.
